About Me

I've always been a bit of a nerd.
Growing up in New England, I had a love of abstract ideas and complex concepts, from sci-fi to cosmology, and that passion eventually led me to studying physics. I've always enjoyed problem-solving, and the logical nature of math, so it seemed like a natural fit. I graduated in 2019 with a B.S. in physics, but finding a job in the industry was another matter entirely.
I decided to pivot to software engineering, as I had taken a few coding classes in school, and enjoyed them immensely. In the spring of 2020, I attended a “coding bootcamp” called Revature, in a full-stack web development program focusing on C# and the .Net tech stack. But with the lockdowns that came that fateful summer, many job opportunities dried up.
I eventually took a supervisor position at Walmart, and ended up learning a lot more than I expected to. I had been a team-lead on 2 group projects at Revature, which certainly helped, but this was a new challenge altogether. This was my first official position of leadership, and it taught me so much about the added responsibilities that came with it.
In early 2022, I heard about a new school, in Tulsa, called Holberton. They have a much more in-depth full-stack program here, where I'm getting a lot of hands-on experience with a number of languages and frameworks, such as Python, Node.js, React, Mysql, Javascript, C, and more.
With my soft skills and technical expertise combined, I offer a unique package. That of a nerd who can program and translate the most complex of data. But also one with enough life experience to relate and communicate with anyone, be it as a team-member or leader. I've always felt like a man with a foot in 2 worlds, in more ways than one, but now I think that just gives me a more well-rounded perspective.
